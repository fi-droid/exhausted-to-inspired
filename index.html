What This Actually Is

A science-aware healing event for
the exhaustion crisis

Exhausted to Inspired is for the person who is tired, tender, curious, grieving, changing, overthinking, creating, rebuilding, or simply needing a softer place to land. The science is clear enough to take seriously: chronic stress affects energy, motivation, attention, mood, relationships, and the body. Social connection, emotional expression, mindfulness, movement, and perceived support can all shape how we handle stress, loneliness, and mental load.

You’ll be guided through practices that help interrupt the exhaustion loop:

  • simple meditation and breathing practices to quiet racing thoughts and help your nervous system settle
  • gentle movement to release tension without needing to be “good” at yoga or perform wellness perfectly
  • creative reflection through art, doodling, and journaling prompts
  • supportive conversations around grief, burnout, political stress, identity, relationships, and uncertainty
  • tarot and intuitive tools used for self-reflection, not prediction or pressure
  • herbs, plants, sound, chanting, and music as grounding practices when words are not enough

What you do not need:

  • you do not need to be spiritual “enough”
  • you do not need to share anything private unless you want to
  • you do not need previous meditation, movement, tarot, or healing experience
  • you do not need to attend every session to benefit
  • you do not need to be in a perfect place emotionally — exhaustion is exactly why this space exists

The Science of Exhaustion

Burnout is not a personality flaw.
It is a signal.

Exhaustion, burnout, overwhelm, and chronic stress are not signs that people are weak. They are signs that the nervous system, attention, emotional capacity, and sense of connection have been under too much load for too long.

✦

Burnout comes from unmanaged chronic stress

The World Health Organization describes burnout as an occupational phenomenon resulting from chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed. That is why this event does not shame exhaustion — it responds to it.

✦

Stress is a whole-person issue

Chronic stress can affect attention, mood, sleep, motivation, emotional reactivity, relationships, and the body. Exhausted to Inspired is built around multiple entry points because stress does not live in only one place.

✦

Connection is protective

The U.S. Surgeon General has identified social connection and community as major influences on health and well-being. That is why this gathering includes live group spaces and lifetime Signal community access.

✦

Support works best when it is structured

The Surgeon General’s workplace well-being framework emphasizes protection from harm, connection, belonging, mattering, and work-life harmony. This event translates those needs into guided, human, online practices.

The promise is not that one event will erase burnout. The promise is that you will have a place to pause, be supported, receive practical tools, reconnect with other humans, and begin moving from exhaustion toward inspiration with less shame and more clarity.

Questions People Actually Have

Before you decide,
here’s the honest version

What does “Exhausted to Inspired” mean?

It means the event is designed for people who are depleted, overwhelmed, burned out, or emotionally tired — and want to move toward clarity, creative energy, connection, and practical support.

What does “healing gathering” mean?

It means a live online event with guided workshops that support reflection, emotional processing, creativity, stress relief, grief support, movement, and connection. It is not therapy, and it is not a medical program. It is a structured community care space built around practices that research often links with stress reduction, emotional processing, and social support.

What does “circle” mean?

A circle is a facilitated group conversation. You may be invited to reflect, listen, share, or simply be present. You are never required to disclose anything personal.

What does “integration” mean?

Integration means taking what you experience and turning it into simple next steps: journaling prompts, practices, reminders, and small changes you can bring into daily life.

Do I need experience with any of this?

No. The sessions are designed to be approachable whether you are brand new or already familiar with meditation, tarot, movement, herbs, art, or healing work.

Can I attend only one session?

Yes. Single-session tickets are $36. This is a good option if one topic feels especially relevant or if you want to try the event without committing to the full gathering.

Is this religious?

No single belief system is required. Some sessions may include spiritual or contemplative practices, but the event is designed to be welcoming, grounded, and consent-based.

What if I cannot afford the ticket?

Please reach out about scholarship support. There are 10 full and partial scholarships available, and no one will be turned away for lack of funds.

Will I have to be on camera?

You can participate at your comfort level. Some sessions may invite interaction, but the overall tone is gentle, spacious, and respectful of different capacities.

Do I get recordings?

Yes. Full-gathering ticket holders get access to all session recordings for 3 days after the event.

Is there community after the event?

Yes. Your ticket includes lifetime access to the online Signal group chat so you can stay connected after the live gathering.

Why does the curation process matter?

Because trust is part of the experience. Fi and Erica chose practitioners they have worked with directly through 1:1 sessions, coaching, guidance, or long-term relationship — so the event is built from real experience, not random booking.
